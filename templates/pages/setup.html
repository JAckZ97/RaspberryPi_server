How to setup a Raspberry Pi

First you need to have a Raspberry Pi, and the one I use is Raspberry Pi zero w version, which is one of the cheapest Raspberry Pi you can find in the market. It looks like down below, and you can buy from HERE ,which only cost you 10 dollar.

You also need an extra SD card for store the OS for your Raspberry Pi and all the other datas, I think 8G will be enough.

Now you are ready to move to software

First you need to Download the OS like Raspbian for your Pi. The reason I choose Raspbian Buster Lite due to its lighter weight, and this is the main reason that I didn't choose Raspbian Buster with desktop. Because I have Pi zero not Pi 3b or 4b, the hardware limitation are there.

After you download the zip file, just decompressed into img file and flash it into the new SD card by balena, which is works for me on MacOS. Download from HERE.

After this, I personally recommand you guys download these two file, which help you to setup ssh and wifi before you insert the SD card on your Raspberry Pi (MAKE SURE YOU CHANGE wpa_supplicant.conf FILE into your personal WIFI name and password, and try to use 2.4GHz WIFI instead of 5GHz). Download HERE

Now you are ready to insert the SD card and boot up your Pi

After insert your card and power it on, you will see it setup by itself and if it ask you about user name and password, it will be "pi" for the user name and "raspberry" for password. Details

You can ssh to your Pi now by following command in your Mac terminal.

"ssh pi@your-pi-ip"

Now you are ready to go!! Congrat!!
